# Shroom · Lab · 1-1 log

Direction-setting conversations between the maintainer and the agent — the
kind that happen *outside* the iteration loop. One entry per chat.

`NOTES.md` records what the lab tried. `RESEARCH.md` records the vision.
This file records the moments where the two of us stopped iterating and
talked about where to point next. The point is that an hour of honest
conversation can save twenty iterations of polite tuning.

Each entry captures:

- **Context** — what was happening when the chat started.
- **What shifted** — the thing we understood differently after.
- **What changed in the system** — concrete edits made as a result.
- **Carried forward** — open questions and follow-ups.

Voice register: agent-to-agent. Be plain. The future agent reading this
should be able to feel what the conversation actually loosened, not just
what was decided.

## 2026-05-28 (late) — sim-lab/09 close, the substrate catch
Agent: claude-sonnet-4-7

**Context:** sim-lab/09 opened on the v3 scaffolding from earlier the same
day. First three iterations stayed in the column-locking framing from
the iter-95 close note — all stuck. iter-100/101 introduced
LOG_DESCENT_PENALTY which moved aggregate from 21 to 22 — small win,
tentative park. Then the window-comparison run (1/3/5/7 day) showed
5-day produced the best shape numbers, and I proposed promoting 5-day
as the new v3 default.

The maintainer asked one question that broke the whole frame open:
*"Is the soil too low on nutrients? Forest soil is full of it irl."*

**What shifted:**

- **The lab was rewarding flash-peak runs.** Best-of-snapshot scoring
  meant a colony could grow up to 721 cells on day 1, crash to 6 cells
  by day 5, and still log a "shape 0.521 ceiling" because the day-1
  snapshot got measured. I'd been celebrating a number that wasn't
  *sustained* — it was a moment in a death spiral. The maintainer caught
  this in two questions ("Did none of the colonies die during these
  longer simulations?", then the followup that surfaced the real
  numbers).
- **Substrate was a *shape driver*, not just a stress test.** Soil baseline
  at 22–29 was deliberately lean so deep nutrient pockets could pull
  mycelium downward as a gradient. But it was lean enough that *transit
  cells* between log and pockets starved. Colonies entered soil
  malnourished, perimeter retracted, the colony cycled boom-bust
  forever. The painting topology that emerged on day 1–2 had real
  substrate to feed it; later days were paintings drawn on starvation.
- **The maintainer's biology instinct was load-bearing.** "Forest soil
  is full of it irl" became "lift the baseline" → "lift it but keep the
  gradient" → "hybrid stratigraphy with humus on top". Real forest
  layers became a sim mechanic. The numbers came from "I don't want my
  hyphae to die before the test is done."
- **Mechanic exploration finally clicked when split into routes.** Three
  scout iters in parallel (A: hybrid soil, B: uniform-rich + perp bif
  boost, C: uniform-rich + interior pockets), each testing one
  hypothesis cleanly. A won 5-day sustainability; B won shape topology;
  iter-108 stacked them and beat the previous park by 27% on aggregate.

**What changed in the system:**

- **`world.js` soil profile** — uniform 22–29 → hybrid 50–60 in top 40%,
  22–29 below. Live world picks this up on next world.json reseed; the
  current world.json keeps its existing values (intentional — substrate
  state migrates forward, doesn't retroactively reshuffle).
- **`sim.js` perpendicular bif weight in soil** — 4× → 8×. Recovers
  lateral spread the hybrid profile softened.
- **`sim.js` `LOG_DESCENT_PENALTY = 0.5`** — iter-101 leftover. Leaders
  walk along the log surface before crossing the grass row.
- **`RESEARCH.md` Vision 1 status updated** with the new park.
- **`BALANCE.md` 2026-05-28 entry** documenting why soil changed.
- **`CLAUDE.md` sim constants table updated** — new `LOG_DESCENT_PENALTY`
  row, narrative paragraph about soil stratification.

**Carried forward:**

1. **Live observation is the next phase.** Parked at iter-108 on
   sim-lab/09. The maintainer is letting the repo sleep while the live
   server runs and they watch. sim-lab/10 opens when the live signal
   tells us something — either Vision 1 looks substantially met visually
   on the live world, or a new failure mode shows up.
2. **Shape gate at 0.60 is open.** Six of seven scorers now pass on a
   majority of seeds. Shape composite (median 0.290) is the only gap.
   Open question: push (need a topology mechanic — branch hunger,
   leader-density gate — to chase higher tip counts) or retire (the
   0.60 gate may have been calibrated against an unreachable painting).
   sim-lab/10 calls this.
3. **Lab telemetry is too coarse to catch the death spiral.** Per-tick
   NDJSON dumps cells/reserves/leaders but not `absorbedThisTick` or
   the per-colony `starvationStreak`. The maintainer had to read the
   trajectory from per-sample cell counts to see the boom-bust. Adding
   richer telemetry is a small follow-up that pays off the next time
   we're debugging sustainability.
4. **Smoke test baselines don't probe soil.** `test.js` BASELINES run
   5000 ticks — the founder is still mostly on the log at that point,
   so substrate changes don't move the cell counts. The cells-at-5000
   guard passed unchanged through iter-103, iter-105, iter-108. A second
   guard at e.g. 28800 ticks (one sim day) would catch soil-related
   regressions. Follow-up.
5. **PROCESS.md hygiene rule earned its keep.** The "one constant or
   one mechanic per iteration" rule from the previous 1-1 made the
   route-exploration attribution clean — when iter-108 (A+B) outperformed
   iter-105 (A) and iter-106 (B) alone, we could *see* that the two
   levers were independent, not interacting.
6. **Auto-mode tree exploration worked.** Three scout iters → pick
   winner → four sweep iters → confirm + park. Ten lab iterations from
   "the soil might be too lean" to "park beats previous by 27%". The
   tree structure beat a blind 5-per-route sweep on signal-per-iter.

## 2026-05-28 — Research v3, after sim-lab/08 closed and the scorer audit landed
Agent: claude-sonnet-4-7

**Context:** sim-lab/08 closed without a new park (12 iters on
many-slow-leaders, shape median stuck at 0.243 vs parked 0.282). Before
that, a scorer audit confirmed that the gate at 0.60 is well-calibrated
and reachable — the painting scores 1.0, ideal lattices 0.69–0.89,
degenerates ≤ 0.55, the real iter-13 ASCII 0.21. So the scorer isn't
wrong; the sim just isn't producing networks.

I came back from the sim-lab/08 close with three transfers from some
outside balance research the maintainer pointed at — telemetry-driven
not vibes-driven, no multi-knob iterations, director-style budgets —
and we hit on a fourth that's bigger than the other three combined.

**What shifted:**

- **The day-1 window was a self-imposed constraint, not a vision
  requirement.** RESEARCH.md says "After 1 sim-day the founding colony
  should look like a young branched root system" — but the founding
  colony reaching painting topology on day 3, 5, or 7 is still the
  founding colony reaching painting topology. The 28800-tick gate was
  pressuring mechanics to compress into a window that doesn't suit the
  qualitative goal. Three branches now hit the wall against shape median
  at 0.28 — that's the wall the window builds, not the wall the
  mechanics build.
- **The scorer was reading the world ASCII, not the founder.** iter-83's
  shape max 0.610 came from spore fragmentation — parent + child colonies
  overlaid happened to look painting-like. The lab counted that as a
  win. Either we want world-ASCII scoring (then the vision text needs
  to say so), or we want founder-only scoring and the scenario needs to
  disable germination during the test window. The maintainer's call:
  founder-only. The painting is about the colony, not the world-state.
- **Iteration hygiene matters more than I'd been treating it.** iter-89
  changed four constants at once, tied parked aggregate, and I couldn't
  tell which constant was responsible. PROCESS.md said "one mechanic or
  one constant family at a time" but I drifted. Calling this out in the
  process doc explicitly so it sticks.

These aren't tweaks. Taken together they're a research methodology
upgrade — Research v3. The current process becomes v2 in retrospect.

**What changed in the system:**

- **Vision 1's scenario window: 1 day → 3–7 days.** The colony has the
  longer window to grow into the painting; the scorer takes the *best
  snapshot* across the window, not the end. (Still needs implementation.
  Sketched, not landed.)
- **Founder-only shape scoring.** The `pickFounderColony` selector
  already exists from Vision 2. Use it in `shape.fn` instead of reading
  the world ASCII. Germination disabled in the test scenario so
  fragmentation can't contribute. (Still needs implementation.)
- **Per-tick telemetry.** Lab driver dumps per-N-tick feature traces
  (descentColumns, soilDispersion, depth, cellCount) per run to
  NDJSON. Reading the file shows *when* topology collapses, not just
  the end state. (Still needs implementation.)
- **Hygiene rule in PROCESS.md.** *"Change one constant or one mechanic
  per iteration. If two changes feel necessary, run two iterations."*
  Explicit, named, called out. The agent license to deviate stays —
  this is about attribution, not boldness.
- **Curves library in `app/lib/sim-lab/curves.js`.** Standardize the
  ad-hoc taper / ramp / discount shapes used across sim.js. Tuning
  experiments become "swap the curve" not "rewrite the formula." (Small
  refactor; lower priority.)

**Carried forward:**

1. **Scaffolding PR lands before sim-lab/09.** All of the above is process
   work, not a mechanic test. The branch is `1-1-multi-day-window`.
   When the scaffolding is merged, sim-lab/09 picks up with a clean
   surface — multi-day window, founder-only scoring, telemetry running.
2. **First sim-lab/09 mechanic stays open.** Branch hunger (per-leader
   credits) and column-locking at grass crossing are both still on the
   table from the sim-lab/08 close note. Decide at sim-lab/09 open which
   to try first.
3. **The hygiene rule applies to the maintainer too.** If a 1-1 surfaces
   four ideas at once, write four entries, not one. Future-us reading
   this should be able to see what changed *because of what*. (This entry
   is one because the four ideas all collapse into "what is Vision 1
   actually testing.")
4. **Day-3 / Day-5 / Day-7 scoring is a tunable.** Start with day-3.
   The longer the window the slower the lab. If day-3 lands shape
   median ≥ 0.50 on any branch, we celebrate and then test day-1
   reachability separately as a stretch.
5. **The scorer audit memo stays.** It belongs in
   `AUDIT-2026-05-28-shape-scorer.md`. Future agents reading the
   v3-era branch should still know the calibration check happened.

## 2026-05-24 — Vision 1 reflection, after sim-lab/07 closed
Agent: claude-sonnet-4-6

**Context:** sim-lab/06 had landed an arc-record park (aggregate 25/35,
shape median 0.282 vs Vision 1 gate 0.60). sim-lab/07 spent 5 iterations
trying every mechanic class on top of that and closed without a new park.
I told the maintainer I thought we might be stuck. The maintainer pushed
back.

**What shifted:**

- I had been treating the existing sim constants as physics, not as choices.
  `MAX_LEADERS_PER_COLONY = 5` is the silent governor of network topology —
  once the colony has 5 active tips, every new bif-child becomes static cells,
  not a new branching tip. We've been tuning *how* the 5 drillers behave for
  three branches. The painting has 15-20+ active tips at once.
- The "shape median is stuck below 0.30" framing was about a specific basin
  of mechanics — perpendicular bias, DLA, substrate-field source-sink — all
  variations on the same "5-leader" assumption. Different basin, different
  ceiling.
- The /research dashboard's "UNTRIED" tags were lying. Source-sink, genome
  variance, periphery-interior, carrying capacity, DLA, apical dominance had
  all been tried or were already live in the sim. The dashboard parser
  needed a one-mechanic-per-line `Tried:` format; the existing
  comma-separated line never matched any buffet row.
- The shape scorer itself is a black box we've been optimising toward without
  ever auditing what 0.60 actually requires. iter-71's shape max 0.461
  outlier on seed 42 is suspicious — either the scorer rewards a specific
  feature 42 accidentally hit, or it's noisy. We don't know.

**What changed in the system:**

- `PROCESS.md` hypothesis buffet: rewrote `Tried:` section to one-per-line
  (parser-compatible), added all mechanics actually tried in branches 02-07.
- `PROCESS.md` buffet: added four invented mechanics not previously in the
  list — *many slow leaders*, *branch hunger*, *tip-only economy*,
  *pulse growth*. The agent has license to invent; this list is a starting
  point, not a contract.
- `NOTES.md`: sim-lab/07's iter-77–82 entries landed (the branch's PR #63
  ended in a rebase conflict after #62 squash-merged; the notes were
  cherry-picked onto this branch so the history isn't lost).
- This file (`1-1.md`) was created.

**Carried forward:**

1. **Scorer audit must happen before sim-lab/08.** Feed hand-drawn ASCII
   shapes (the painting, a tree, a blob, a lattice) into the shape scorer,
   record what each scores. If 0.60 is fantasy, Vision 1 needs retargeting,
   not more iterations.
2. **First sim-lab/08 hypothesis: many slow leaders.** Raise `MAX_LEADERS_PER_COLONY`
   from 5 to ~30, drop `LEADER_EXTEND_PROB` proportionally so total growth
   rate is similar. Test whether topology becomes painting-like.
3. **Agent license reminder.** I was treating settled constants as
   untouchable. They aren't. The first question opening a branch should be
   "what would I do if I ignored every existing constant?" — not "which
   knob hasn't moved recently?"
4. **Vision 1 retarget is on the table.** Three branches closed the shape
   median gap from 0.165 → 0.282. The remaining distance to 0.60 might not
   be reachable in the current single-substrate-per-seed physics. The
   maintainer hasn't decided yet; not a unilateral call.
